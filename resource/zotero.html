So you think you know how to use Zotero?

Part I

Author

Barboza-Salerno

Introduction

I asked you to download Zotero as part of this week’s assignments. Zotero is a powerful reference management system. I am aware that those of you who use it have a good idea how to manage your references in Word. However, there are a lot of powerful features that most people probably are not aware of. I will go through some of these in this document.

Link Zotero to an online portal

Sometimes you want to share files with others or simply want to access your Zotero files from another account. You can do this easily if you have an online Zotero account.

Go to Zotero.org and click “Register for a free account.” Remember your username and password.

Open Zotero. Click Edit –> Preferences Enter your username and password as below

The click “Set up syncing”

You can choose your libraries or you can choose all libraries, whatever you wish.

Creating Zotero Groups to Share Libraries

I have created a Zotero group so you can practice.

To create a new group for your team, Click on “Groups” then “Create a new group” as below

Choose a name for your group and then select membership type. Click “Create Group”

You can invite group members. On the Group Settings page, select “Member Settings.” To invite members, click on Send More Invitations

Once your group is created, there will be a corresponding Group Folder in your Zotero Library

Add references to your group collections (folders).

This works just like other Zotero folders—drag your references from your Library and place them into the Group Library

Note

PhD students, set up a Zotero Group for your research project. If you are collaborating, only one person needs to do this.

Add your publications to Zotero and create a Zotero profile

For the most part, your publications are available from Google Scholar.

Note

If you have published work, and do not have a google profile, set one up now.

To demonstrate I will use my profile which is here.

Click on the checkbox next to the word “Title” like below

Click “Export” select “BibTex” and then “Export all my articles.”

If you are using Microsoft Edge, you will see something that looks like this

These are the references in BibTex format. Right click in the webpage and “Save As” … (name it something).

Now, import the file into Zotero as follows:

Go to Zotero, and from the file menu select File -> Import and follow the instructions below.

A library will be created that has the same name as the imported file.

Create a Zotero Profile

In Zotero, you see a library called “My Publications”

Go to the library that has your publications and highlight them (Click Ctl-A on Windows). Drag them into your “My Publications” folder.

Now, go back to the Zotero webpage, and click on your name and then “My Profile.” After dragging your publications into the folder, you will be asked to verify you created this work. Once you do that, your profile should go from this
